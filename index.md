# Nathan Sauldubois

{: .lead}
Postdoctoral Researcher in Applied Mathematics, Financial Risk Engineering Department, NYU Tandon School of Engineering.

I am a postdoctoral researcher in applied mathematics at NYU Tandon School of Engineering, in the Financial Risk Engineering department. My research is in mathematical finance, with a focus on distributionally robust optimization, optimal transport, adapted Wasserstein geometry, model risk, martingale constraints, semi-static hedging, and BSDE/RBSDE sensitivity analysis.

My work studies quantitative questions where uncertainty, constraints, and dynamic information structures play a central role. I am particularly interested in methods that connect robust finance, optimal transport, stochastic analysis, and numerical approximation.
